Lake Mutanda

Often called "the most beautiful lake in Uganda," Lake Mutanda is a pristine crater lake surrounded by the dramatic peaks of the Virunga volcanoes. Its calm, crystal-clear waters reflect the towering silhouettes of Mount Muhabura, Gahinga, and Sabyinyo.

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is one of Africa's most ancient "afromontane" rainforests, dating back over 25,000 years. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to nearly half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas - approximately 459 individuals in 50 habituated groups.

The "impenetrable" forest earned its name from its dense undergrowth of herbs, vines, and shrubs. Walking through Bwindi is like stepping back in time, with towering trees draped in moss and a rich symphony of bird calls echoing through the mist.

Highlights

  • Home to 459 mountain gorillas
  • 4 gorilla trekking sectors
  • 350+ bird species
  • 10 primate species
  • Ancient forest ecosystem
  • Batwa cultural experiences

Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

Uganda's smallest national park packs an extraordinary punch. Mgahinga Gorilla National Park protects the Ugandan slopes of three dormant volcanoes - Muhabura, Gahinga, and Sabyinyo - that form part of the Virunga Massif.

Beyond gorilla trekking, Mgahinga is the best place in Uganda to track the endangered golden monkeys, with their striking golden-orange fur. The park also offers challenging volcano hikes with rewards of panoramic views across three countries.

Highlights

  • 1 habituated gorilla group (Nyakagezi)
  • Best golden monkey tracking
  • 3 volcano hiking trails
  • Garama Cave exploration
  • Batwa Trail experience
  • Stunning volcanic scenery
Distance from Lodge 30 minutes drive
Best Time to Visit Year-round

Mountain Adventure

Virunga Mountains

The Virunga Mountains are a chain of eight volcanic peaks stretching along the border of Uganda, Rwanda, and DR Congo. These dramatic peaks, ranging from 3,474m to 4,507m, are the stuff of legend - formed by volcanic activity and now cloaked in lush montane forest.

Climbing these volcanoes offers some of East Africa's most rewarding hiking experiences, with diverse ecosystems from bamboo forests to alpine meadows, and summit views that span three countries.

Hikeable Peaks

  • Mount Muhabura - 4,127m (most challenging)
  • Mount Sabyinyo - 3,669m (three countries summit)
  • Mount Gahinga - 3,474m (crater swamp)
Trek Duration 6-10 hours round trip
Fitness Level Challenging

Africa's Second Deepest Lake

Lake Bunyonyi

Lake Bunyonyi, meaning "place of many little birds," lives up to its name with over 200 bird species recorded. This stunning lake, at 1,962m elevation, is dotted with 29 islands, each with its own history and character.

The lake's terraced hillsides, carved by the Bakiga people over generations, create one of Uganda's most photographed landscapes. At up to 900m deep in places, it's Africa's second deepest lake and one of the few in Uganda that's bilharzia-free.

Highlights

  • 29 islands to explore
  • 200+ bird species
  • Safe swimming (bilharzia-free)
  • Dugout canoe rides
  • Community village visits
  • Stunning terraced landscapes
Distance from Lodge 2 hours drive
