About Us

A Sanctuary in the
Heart of Africa

Nestled at the foothills of the Virunga Mountains in Kisoro, Uganda, Silverback Haven offers an unparalleled escape into one of Earth's most biodiverse regions. Our lodge serves as your gateway to encountering the magnificent mountain gorillas of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Mgahinga Gorilla National Park.

We believe in sustainable tourism that benefits local communities and protects the endangered wildlife that makes this region so extraordinary. Every stay at Silverback Haven contributes to conservation efforts and community development programs.

Wildlife Experiences

Encounter the
Extraordinary

Kisoro is home to half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas. Our expert guides will lead you on unforgettable journeys into their realm.

Gorilla Trekking

Trek through the misty bamboo forests of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park or Mgahinga to spend an hour in the presence of a habituated gorilla family. A life-changing experience with the gentle giants of Uganda.

  • 4-8 hours
  • Moderate to challenging
  • Max 8 people per group
Book Experience

Golden Monkey Tracking

Discover the playful and endangered golden monkeys in the bamboo zones of Mgahinga Gorilla National Park. Watch these beautiful red-gold primates leap and play in their natural habitat.

  • 2-4 hours
  • Easy to moderate
  • Max 12 people per group
Book Experience

Bird Watching Safari

With over 350 bird species, including 23 Albertine Rift endemics, Kisoro is a birder's paradise. Spot the Rwenzori Turaco, Handsome Francolin, and many more rare species.

  • Half or full day
  • Easy
  • Equipment provided
Book Experience

Virunga Volcano Hiking

Climb the majestic volcanoes of the Virunga range in Uganda. Summit Mount Muhabura, Gahinga, or Sabyinyo for breathtaking views across Uganda, Rwanda, and DR Congo.

  • 6-10 hours
  • Challenging
  • 3,474m - 4,127m elevation

Cultural Immersion

Meet the Batwa
Forest People

The Batwa are the original forest dwellers of the Virunga region in Uganda. For thousands of years, they lived in harmony with the mountain gorillas, sharing the ancient forests as their home.

Join us for an authentic cultural experience as the Batwa share their traditional songs, dances, and ancient forest knowledge. Your visit directly supports their community development programs.
