Our Story

A Dream Born in
the Mist

Silverback Haven was born from a deep love for Uganda's extraordinary wildlife and a belief that tourism can be a force for good. Founded in 2009 by a team of conservationists and hospitality experts, our lodge was designed from the ground up to provide world-class accommodation while supporting the protection of endangered mountain gorillas.

Nestled at the foothills of the Virunga Mountains in Kisoro, we chose this location not just for its breathtaking beauty, but for its proximity to both Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Mgahinga Gorilla National Park - home to more than half of the world's remaining mountain gorillas.

Over the years, Silverback Haven has welcomed thousands of guests from around the world, each leaving with a deeper appreciation for these gentle giants and the fragile ecosystem they call home. We've also grown our community programs, employing over 150 local staff and supporting schools, healthcare initiatives, and conservation projects throughout the region.

Cultural Partner
Cultural Heritage

The Batwa
Forest People

The Batwa are the original inhabitants of the Virunga forests. For thousands of years, they lived in harmony with the mountain gorillas, sharing the ancient forests as their home. When the forests became protected national parks, the Batwa were displaced and lost their traditional way of life.

At Silverback Haven, we work closely with Batwa communities to preserve their heritage and create sustainable livelihoods. Our Batwa Cultural Trail experience directly supports their community development programs, including education, healthcare, and housing.
